OVERSEAS TRAVEL TO AUSTRALIA

Each year Australia welcomes a large number of arrivals into the country. However, due to the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic and its resultant travel restrictions across the world, these numbers have significantly dropped.
This visualisation exhibits the various monthly statistics of the arrival numbers for the years 2019 to 2021, to give an outlook of the impact of COVID-19 on overseas travel.

AUSTRALIAN STATE-WISE DISTRIBUTION OF ARRIVALS

The choropleth map below showcases the number of arrivals in different states of Australia, for the month of June in the years 2019 to 2021.

In June 2021, Australia saw 103,500 arrivals, a sharp decrease of 12,000 trips when compared with previous month. A 93.4 per cent decrease in June arrivals was observed for the year 2021 as compared to pre-COVID travels in 2019.

The 'Year' slider can be used to view the number of arrivals in the Australian states for the years 2019 to 2021 in the month of June.

ARRIVALS ACCORDING TO CITIZENSHIP

An estimated number of 40,500 Australian citizen arrivals and 9,100 permanent visa holders were welcomed back into the country, in the month of June 2021. Majority of the Non-Australian arrivals comprised of New Zealand citizens (42%) at 43,800. These arrival numbers were significantly higher then the rest of the countries because of the travel bubble between Australia and New Zealand, which allowed quarantine-free travel between the two countries.

COUNTRY-WISE AND VISA-TYPE DISTRIBUTION OF ARRIVALS

This stacked bar chart showcases the list of countries with the most arrivals in Australia, in the month of June for the years 2019 to 2021. The top three countries of most arrivals are New Zealand, UK and China, at 43,800, 2,200 and 1,900 arrivals, respectively.

The legend can be clicked to view specific year information.

The visa type of the largest number of arrivals for pre-COVID travel was Temporary Visa in 2019. However since 2020, because of the emerging of the COVID-19 pandemic threats, Australia has multiple overseas travel restrictions in place, which resulted in significant decrease of arrivals in both the visa types.

The three largest visa groups arriving in Australia in June 2021 were Temporary Other Visas, Permanent Skilled Visas and Temporary Visitor Visas, at 5,400, 3,600 and 3,400 arrivals, respectively.
